MARVEL — Mars Methane Analysis & Remote Vehicle for Exploration and Learning

MARVEL is a conceptual Mars exploration mission designed to resolve inconsistencies in methane detection observed by prior missions (Curiosity, TGO). The mission integrates a low-Mars-orbit satellite with advanced spectrometers and a surface exploration vehicle capable of sampling atmospheric and subsurface methane. In this MAE 460 course, our group initiated a 4-stage Systems review (SRR, PDR, CDR, FDR) with the help of our mentor, Steve Matousek- Mars Exploration Program Advanced Studies Office Manager @ NASA JPL, and our Professor, Javid Bayandor.

The primary objective is to locate, characterize, and determine the origin (biotic vs abiotic) of methane emissions on Mars while operating within realistic cost, schedule, and technology constraints consistent with NASA mission planning.

Technical Highlights

  • Mission Architecture: Orbiter + surface vehicle (rover / aerial sampling concepts)
  • Science Payload: Multi-channel IR & UV-VIS spectrometers for CH₄ and hydrocarbon detection
  • Orbit Design: ~300 km circular Mars orbit for improved spatial resolution
  • Propulsion: Hall-effect thrusters + bipropellant attitude control
  • Power System: Multi-junction GaAs solar arrays + Li-ion batteries
  • ADCS: Reaction wheels, star trackers, sun sensors, IMU (with redundancy)
  • Sampling Concepts: Atmospheric sorbent tubes, subsurface drilling, ground-penetrating radar
  • Analysis Depth: Mass, power, thermal, orbital period, ground coverage, and SNR considerations
